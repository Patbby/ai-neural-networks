EfficientNetV2

Smaller Models and Faster Training

A family of convolutional neural networks that combines training-aware Neural Architecture Search (NAS) and compound scaling to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy with significantly faster training speed and smaller model size.

Authors: Mingxing Tan, Quoc V. Le
Published: ICML 2021
Institution: Google Research

Key Innovations

Fused-MBConv

Replaces depthwise separable convolutions in early layers with standard 3×3 convolutions followed by 1×1 convolutions, dramatically improving training speed on modern accelerators.

Training-Aware NAS

Neural Architecture Search that jointly optimizes for accuracy, training speed, and parameter efficiency using a weighted product objective.

Progressive Learning

Dynamic adjustment of image size and regularization during training—starting with small images and weak regularization, gradually increasing both.

Fused-MBConv Architecture

The key building block of EfficientNetV2. Unlike the original MBConv which uses depthwise separable convolutions, Fused-MBConv uses a standard 3×3 convolution followed by 1×1 pointwise convolution, significantly improving training speed on GPUs/TPUs.

MBConv vs Fused-MBConv

Original MBConv (EfficientNetV1)

  • • Uses depthwise separable convolution (3×3)
  • • Low FLOPs but poor GPU utilization
  • • High memory access cost per computation

Fused-MBConv (EfficientNetV2)

NEW
  • ✓ Uses standard 3×3 convolution (fused)
  • ✓ Superior GPU/TPU utilization
  • ✓ Up to 11× faster training speed

Progressive Learning Strategy

Unlike traditional training with fixed image sizes, EfficientNetV2 gradually increases image size and regularization strength during training. This approach improves both training speed and final accuracy.

Training-Aware Neural Architecture Search

Unlike traditional NAS that only optimizes for accuracy and FLOPs, EfficientNetV2's training-aware NAS jointly optimizes for training speed, accuracy, and parameter efficiency.
