MAE

Masked Autoencoders Are Scalable Vision Learners

Kaiming He, Xinlei Chen, Saining Xie, Yanghao Li, Piotr Dollár, Ross Girshick

Facebook AI Research (FAIR)

📄 Paper (arXiv) 💻 Code (PyTorch) 📑 PDF

📋 Abstract

This paper shows that masked autoencoders (MAE) are scalable self-supervised learners for computer vision. Our MAE approach is simple: we mask random patches of the input image and reconstruct the missing pixels. It is based on two core designs. First, we develop an asymmetric encoder-decoder architecture, with an encoder that operates only on the visible subset of patches (without mask tokens), along with a lightweight decoder that reconstructs the original image from the latent representation and mask tokens. Second, we find that masking a high proportion of the input image, e.g., 75%, yields a nontrivial and meaningful self-supervisory task. Coupling these two designs enables us to train large models efficiently and effectively: we accelerate training (by 3× or more) and improve accuracy. Our scalable approach allows for learning high-capacity models that generalize well.

🎯 Problem Statement

Before MAE, self-supervised learning in computer vision was dominated by contrastive methods that require:

  • Large batch sizes for negative sampling
  • Complex data augmentations
  • Extra encoder networks (momentum encoders)
  • Significant computational resources

Meanwhile, in NLP, BERT demonstrated that masked autoencoding — simply predicting missing tokens — could learn powerful representations without negative samples or complex augmentations. Could the same simple approach work for images?

The challenge: vision tasks involve continuous, high-dimensional pixel data, unlike discrete language tokens. Previous attempts at masked autoencoding in vision produced inferior results compared to contrastive methods.

💡 Key Innovation

MAE introduces two core designs that make masked autoencoding work effectively for vision:

🏗️ Asymmetric Encoder-Decoder

The encoder processes only visible patches (without mask tokens), making it more efficient. A lightweight decoder then reconstructs the full image from latent representations and mask tokens.

🎭 High Masking Ratio (75%)

Masking 75% of input patches creates a nontrivial task that forces the model to learn semantic understanding rather than local interpolation. This is much higher than BERT's 15% masking ratio.

The MAE Philosophy

Mask → Encode → Decode → Reconstruct
Simple recipe: BERT-style pretraining for Vision Transformers

🏗️ Architecture

MAE Architecture Overview

Key Architectural Insights

🎯 Asymmetric Design

The encoder is heavy (ViT-Large/Huge), but processes only 25% of patches. The decoder is lightweight (8 blocks, 512-dim) but handles all patches.

🔮 No Mask Tokens in Encoder

Unlike BERT, MAE doesn't feed mask tokens to the encoder. This reduces computation by ~75% during pretraining!

📐 Pixel-level Reconstruction

The decoder reconstructs raw pixels (normalized), not features. This forces the encoder to learn high-level semantic representations.

🎭 Why 75% Masking?

MAE's high masking ratio (75%) is counterintuitive but crucial. Here's why:

Masking Ratio Ablation

With 75% masking, the model cannot rely on local pixel correlations. It must develop a holistic understanding of the image content — object shapes, textures, and semantic context — to successfully reconstruct the masked regions.

🌍 Impact & Legacy

Why MAE Matters

MAE demonstrated that simplicity wins in self-supervised learning:

  • No negative samples — Unlike contrastive methods, MAE doesn't need large batches or negative pairs
  • No data augmentation — Random masking is the only "augmentation" needed
  • Scalable — The approach scales naturally to larger models and datasets
  • Efficient — 3× faster training than contrastive methods due to asymmetric design

Influence on Subsequent Work

🔬 Video MAE

Extended MAE to video understanding with spatiotemporal masking, achieving SOTA on video recognition.

🎨 Data2Vec

Unified self-supervised learning across vision, speech, and NLP using MAE-style reconstruction.

🖼️ BEiT, SimMIM, CAE

Variants and improvements on masked image modeling, validating the MAE approach.

🌐 Multi-modal

Masked modeling extended to vision-language (FLAVA, VLMo) and beyond.

✨ Conclusion

MAE proved that less is more in self-supervised visual learning. By embracing extreme masking (75%) and an asymmetric encoder-decoder design, MAE achieved:

  • State-of-the-art performance on ImageNet with only 1K data
  • Superior transfer learning across detection, segmentation, and classification
  • 3× training efficiency compared to contrastive methods
  • A simpler, more scalable approach to visual pretraining

MAE's success validated a core hypothesis: vision and language can share the same learning paradigm. Just as BERT revolutionized NLP through masked language modeling, MAE brought the same elegant simplicity to computer vision.
