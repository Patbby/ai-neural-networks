🛣️ Highway Networks

Training Very Deep Networks with Information Highways

📄 arXiv 2015 👤 Srivastava, Greff, Schmidhuber 🏢 IDSIA 📊 3,000+ Citations
📄 Paper

📋 Overview

Highway Networks, introduced by Srivastava et al. in 2015, were a groundbreaking architecture designed to ease gradient-based training of very deep neural networks. The key innovation was the introduction of gating units that learn to regulate the flow of information through the network, allowing unimpeded information flow across several layers on "information highways." This architecture predates and inspired ResNet's skip connections, enabling the training of networks with hundreds of layers.

Key Innovation: Unlike standard feedforward networks where y = H(x, W_H), Highway Networks introduce a transform gate T(x, W_T) and a carry gate C(x, W_C) to regulate information flow, allowing the network to pass information directly through layers when beneficial.

🎯 Core Innovation: The Highway Layer

The Highway Network architecture modifies the traditional neural network layer by introducing gating mechanisms inspired by LSTM cells. Each layer can now control how much of its input passes through unchanged (via the "carry" gate) versus how much gets transformed (via the "transform" gate).

📐 Mathematical Formulation

Standard Layer

y = H(x, W_H)
Where H is a nonlinear transformation (e.g., ReLU, tanh)

Highway Layer

y = T(x, W_T) · H(x, W_H) + (1 - T(x, W_T)) · x
Where T is the transform gate output (typically σ(W_T · x + b_T)), ranging from 0 to 1
Key Insight: When T = 0, the layer acts as an identity mapping (y = x), passing information directly through. When T = 1, it acts like a standard layer (y = H(x)). The network learns to balance these behaviors across layers.

Gate Activation Function

T(x) = σ(W_T · x + b_T)
The transform gate uses sigmoid activation to output values between 0 and 1

🧠 Key Innovations

🚗 Information Highways

Direct identity mappings that allow information to flow across many layers without being transformed, preserving gradients and enabling very deep networks.

🎛️ Adaptive Gating

Transform gates T(x) learn to balance between transformation (H) and direct passage (identity), allowing each layer to decide how much to process vs. pass through.

📉 Bias Initialization

The bias of transform gates is typically initialized to negative values (e.g., -2), making the network initially act like an identity function (T ≈ 0), then learning to open gates as needed.

🔄 Gradient Flow

Unlike plain networks where gradients vanish through many layers, Highway Networks preserve gradient flow through the carry paths, enabling training of 100+ layer networks.

🔄 Evolution: From Highway Networks to ResNet

Highway Networks were a crucial stepping stone in the development of very deep architectures. They demonstrated that:

  • Networks with 100+ layers could be trained with SGD
  • Learned skip connections (gated) were beneficial
  • The "carry" mechanism preserved gradients effectively
ResNet Connection: ResNet (2016), published a year later, took Highway Networks' concept and simplified it. Instead of learned gates (T and 1-T), ResNet uses fixed identity shortcuts (always T=0.5, i.e., y = H(x) + x). This simplification worked surprisingly well and became the dominant approach for deep networks.
The evolution from Highway Networks to ResNet. Highway Networks introduced the concept of learned skip connections in 2015, which ResNet simplified to fixed identity mappings in 2016. ResNet's simpler approach became dominant, but Highway Networks were the pioneering work that proved very deep networks were trainable.

📚 Technical Details

Initialization Strategy

The key to successful Highway Network training lies in proper initialization of the transform gate bias:

b_T = -2 (typically)
With sigmoid activation: σ(-2) ≈ 0.12, meaning the network initially passes ~88% of information through unchanged

This initialization strategy ensures that:

  • The network starts by acting as a shallow network (mostly identity mappings)
  • As training progresses, layers learn to "open" their gates (increase T)
  • Depth is utilized gradually, avoiding the vanishing gradient problem early in training

🎯 Impact and Legacy

🏗️ Architectural Innovation

First architecture to successfully train networks with 100+ layers using SGD, proving that depth alone wasn't the limiting factor.

📖 Inspired ResNet

The conceptual foundation for ResNet's skip connections. ResNet simplified the gating mechanism while maintaining the core insight.

🔬 Research Direction

Opened research into how neural networks can learn to route information, influencing architectures like DenseNet and attention mechanisms.

🧠 Biological Inspiration

The gating mechanism concept from LSTMs showed that neural architectures could benefit from explicit control mechanisms beyond simple feedforward computation.

📖 Summary

Highway Networks (2015) were a groundbreaking contribution to deep learning that demonstrated how very deep neural networks could be trained effectively. By introducing adaptive gating mechanisms inspired by LSTMs, they allowed information to flow directly across multiple layers through "information highways." This innovation was the conceptual precursor to ResNet and proved that depth was not inherently a limiting factor for neural network performance. The paper's key insight—that learned skip connections could enable stable gradient flow through 100+ layer networks—reshaped the field's understanding of network architecture design and opened the door to the era of very deep learning.
