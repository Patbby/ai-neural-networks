Constitutional AI

Constitutional AI (CAI) trains harmless AI systems using a set of explicit principles ("the constitution") with AI feedback instead of human labels — a scalable approach to RLHF that reduces reliance on human annotation for harmful content evaluation.

Organization: Anthropic
Year: 2022
Method: RLAIF
Model: Claude (internal)

Core Concept

Standard RLHF requires human annotators to label model outputs as harmful or helpful — an expensive, psychologically taxing process that doesn't scale well to diverse harm types. Constitutional AI replaces much of this with AI-generated feedback guided by explicit principles, forming a closed-loop training process.

The Two-Phase Training Process

1

Supervised Learning from AI Critique (SL-CAI)

The model is prompted with harmful questions, generates an initial response, then critiques its own response against a constitutional principle and revises it. This supervised revision data is used for SFT fine-tuning.

2

RL from AI Feedback (RLAIF)

A separate feedback model — also guided by the constitution — generates preference labels between pairs of responses. These AI-generated labels train a reward model, which is then used for PPO fine-tuning. No human harmfulness labels needed.

★

The Constitution

A short list (~16) of explicit principles drawn from sources like the UN Declaration of Human Rights, Anthropic's usage policies, and common ethical guidelines. These are embedded as prompts in the critique and feedback phases.

Why Constitutional AI Matters

📜

Explicit Transparency

The model's values are encoded in a readable document (the constitution) rather than implicitly absorbed from thousands of human labels — making the alignment process auditable and adjustable.

📈

Scalable Alignment

By using AI feedback instead of human feedback for harm detection, CAI removes the most expensive and psychologically difficult bottleneck in RLHF — labeling harmful content at scale.

⚖️

Helpfulness-Harmlessness Balance

Prior RLHF-trained models learned to refuse borderline requests excessively to be "safe." CAI models achieve better harmlessness without degrading helpfulness on legitimate tasks.

🔁

Self-Improvement Loop

The critique-revision cycle is itself a form of chain-of-thought reasoning about ethics, which improves the model's reasoning quality in addition to reducing harmful outputs.

Training Pipeline Details

Identify specific problematic parts." Response A: [option_a] Response B: [option_b] Constitutional principle: [relevant principle] Answer: [AI generates preference label A or B]

🔑 Key Finding: AI Labels ≈ Human Labels for Harmlessness

The paper showed that AI-generated preference labels using constitutional principles correlated strongly with human preference labels for harmlessness. This validated the core hypothesis that explicit principles + capable AI can replace human annotators for safety alignment.

Impact & Legacy

Constitutional AI became the foundation of Claude's training pipeline at Anthropic. The "RLAIF" (RL from AI Feedback) paradigm it introduced has since been adopted widely — Google's Gemini used similar AI-feedback mechanisms, and the approach influenced how the entire field thinks about scalable alignment.

The explicit constitution framing contributed to Anthropic's positioning around interpretable, transparent AI alignment — in contrast to methods that implicitly encode values through massive human labeling. The paper also introduced the important finding that models trained with CAI show less unhelpful over-refusal than models trained purely on human harmlessness labels.
